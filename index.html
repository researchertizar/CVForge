👤 Contact Info

Your name and contact details go at the very top of your CV. Keep it clean and professional.

📌 Quick tips: Use a professional email (firstname.lastname@gmail.com). Add city + country — not your full home address. LinkedIn is important — many ATS systems verify it.
💬 Write your name exactly as it appears on official documents.
💬 A one-line label that appears under your name. Leave blank to auto-generate.
💬 Use a professional email — avoid nicknames or numbers like "cooldev99".
💬 Include your country code (e.g. +91 for India, +44 for UK).
💬 City and country only — never your full home address on a CV.
💬 Paste your LinkedIn profile link. Many recruiters check this directly.
💬 Important for developers — shows your actual code and projects.
💬 Personal website, portfolio, or Behance link if relevant to your role.

✍️ Professional Summary

A short, powerful paragraph at the top of your CV that tells recruiters who you are in 3–4 sentences.

⚡ Why it matters: Recruiters spend 6–10 seconds scanning a CV. This is what they read first. The AI will write it for you using keywords from your job description — just fill in the context below.
Background (for AI context)
💬 Enter 0 if you're a fresher. This helps the AI set the right tone.
💬 Your main area of expertise or what you're best known for professionally.
💬 List your most important skills separated by commas. Copy exact names from the job posting — "React.js" not "React".
💬 Brief bullet points about your best work. AI will weave these into your CV as quantified achievements.

Add your jobs starting with the most recent one first. Use the AI button inside each entry to improve your bullet points.

⚡ Skills

List your technical skills, tools, and methods. The AI can auto-fill these from your job description.

🔍 Exact match matters: ATS systems do literal text matching. "Node.js" and "NodeJS" are different strings. Click "Extract from Job Description" to auto-fill skills using exact names from the posting you pasted.
Skills Library
💬 Your core technical skills. Type one or paste multiple separated by commas, then press Enter or click Add.
💬 Software tools, cloud platforms, databases, and services you regularly use.
💬 Development approaches, architecture patterns, and technical concepts you know well.
💬 Limit to 3–4 genuinely relevant ones. "Hardworking" and "passionate" add no value — be specific.
💬 Include proficiency level in brackets. Only list if relevant to the role or if you're multilingual.

🚀 Projects

Personal or professional projects show your initiative. Especially valuable if you're a fresher or changing careers.

📊 Make it count: Include the tech stack (exact names), what it does, and any real impact — users, downloads, stars on GitHub. Even solo university or freelance projects count. Add GitHub/live links if available.
+ Add project

🎓 Education

Add your degrees, diplomas, and qualifications. Most recent first.

🏫 Format matters: Write the full degree name — "Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering" not "B.Tech Mech". Include CGPA only if it's strong (above 7.5/10 or 3.5/4.0 or 2:1 equivalent).
+ Add education

🏆 Certifications

Online courses, industry certificates, and professional qualifications. These show continuous learning.

📑 What to include: Coursera, Udemy, Google, AWS, Microsoft, and similar certificates all count. Use the official name (e.g. "Python for Everybody Specialization – Coursera" not just "Python Course").
+ Add certification
Additional Information
💬 Academic prizes, competition wins, or recognition from employers. Adds credibility.
💬 Shows character and involvement beyond work. Especially valuable for freshers.
💬 Professional bodies or associations you belong to. Optional but adds credibility.
